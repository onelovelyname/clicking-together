Our Mission

We live in a world where communities of people hold prejudices against one another. India’s caste system signals a person’s place in society – their worth, their jobs, their history. In much of the United States, given the string of events from September 11 to the rise of ISIS attacks in recent years, some hold strong prejudice against people of particular looks. Sikhs who wear turbans, Muslim women who wear hijabs, and many more types of people are assumed to be the same – somehow linked to terrorist networks. In almost all parts of the world, the wealthy and the poor live side-by-side – whether it be a household wife with her maid or a gated community a few minutes from a favela. We rarely have conversations with one another, nor do we fully understand who each other are and how each other live.

Clicking Together aims to break boundaries and prejudices. Our mission is to develop the next generation of empathetic citizens who seek to understand people who are different.

Our Team

Hannah Yang

Founder

Hannah was born in Taiwan and moved to Los Angeles, California at the age of 10. She is interested in social entrepreneurship — how business can be used to alleviate poverty. She has done development internships in Guatemala and Peru and traveled extensively in Southeast Asia to learn more about the role of social entrepreneurship in international development. In college, she started a socially-responsible t-shirt business EthiCAL Apparel and taught a course on Social Entrepreneurship. In addition to social entrepreneurship, she is passionate about creative writing, photography, and videography.

Hannah studied International Development Studies and Business Administration at the University of California, Berkeley. She now works as an analyst in a consulting firm in San Francisco.

Shiv Tandan

Founder

Shiv is a recent graduate from National University of Singapore – much to everyone’s surprise, he studied Industrial and Systems Engineering. Instead of studying, he ended up spending most of his time in the theatre and cramped dorm rooms shooting his films. He was an enthusiastic member and twice the President of NUS Stage. It was with NUS Stage that he directed a musical, and then wrote and acted in The Good the Bad and the Sholay, which won him a nomination for Best Script at the Life! Theatre Awards in Singapore. He’s also co-founder and creative lead of The Film Guys, a film start-up.

Shiv has always been interested in education, and how he can contribute to making things a little better. Clicking Together affords him the opportunity to work at the intersection of three areas that are close to his heart – photography/film, education, and community building.

Charmaine Poh

Founder

Charmaine Poh was born and raised in Singapore. She moved to Boston for college, where she studied International Relations and Communications and Media Studies at Tufts University. While in college, she co-founded the Tufts chapter of Love146, an international non-profit that fights child sex trafficking. She was also deeply involved in the Program for Narrative and Documentary Practice, which took her to the streets of Yangon, Burma, and Dhaka, Bangladesh, where she documented various stories.

Charmaine is now back in Singapore, and continues to write and photograph. Sometimes she posts things at http://psxcharmaine.wordpress.com/.
